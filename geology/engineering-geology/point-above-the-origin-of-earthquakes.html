Point above the origin of earthquakes

(A) ** Epicenter

(B) Magma chamber

(C) Focus

(D) Hot spot

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The point where the earthquake originates is called the seismic focus. The point on the Earth’s surface vertically above the focus is called its epicentre.

Concept note-2: -The location below the earth’s surface where the earthquake starts is called the hypocenter, and the location directly above it on the surface of the earth is called the epicenter.

Concept note-3: -Epicenter is the location on the surface of the Earth directly above where the earthquake starts. Focus (aka Hypocenter) is the location in the Earth where the earthquake starts.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is Focus. The focus is the point within the earth where an earthquake rupture starts. It is also termed as the hypocenter. The epicenter is the point on the earth’s surface vertically above the hypocenter.

Concept note-5: -The point inside the Earth where an earthquake originates is known as the focus (or hypocenter) of the earthquake. The point directly above the focus at the surface of the earth is called the epicenter of the earthquake.
