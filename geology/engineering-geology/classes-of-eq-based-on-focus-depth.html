classes of EQ based on focus depth.

(A) Surface

(B) ** Shallow

(C) ** Intermediate

(D) ** Deep

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Shallow earthquakes are between 0 and 70 km deep; intermediate earthquakes, 70-300 km deep; and deep earthquakes, 300-700 km deep. In general, the term “deep-focus earthquakes” is applied to earthquakes deeper than 70 km.

Concept note-2: -Earthquake-Shallow, intermediate, and deep foci | Britannica.

Concept note-3: -The focus is the place inside Earth’s crust where an earthquake originates. The point on the Earth’s surface directly above the focus is the epicenter.

Concept note-4: -Shallow depth of field keeps a small area of your photo in focus and blurs the rest, whereas deep depth of field maintains focus throughout the photo. You can adapt focus with depth of field by changing your aperture, length of your lens, and your subject’s position.
