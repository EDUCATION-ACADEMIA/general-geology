Which of these are metamorphic rocks? Choose 2

(A) Limestone

(B) ** Marble

(C) ** Gneiss

(D) Granite

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Common metamorphic rocks include phyllite, schist, gneiss, quartzite and marble.

Concept note-2: -Gneiss is a coarse to medium grained banded metamorphic rock formed from igneous or sedimentary rocks during regional metamorphism. Rich in feldspars and quartz, gneisses also contain mica minerals and aluminous or ferromagnesian silicates.

Concept note-3: -Metamorphic rocks are divided into two categories-Foliates and Non-foliates. Foliates are composed of large amounts of micas and chlorites. These minerals have very distinct cleavage. Foliated metamorphic rocks will split along cleavage lines that are parallel to the minerals that make up the rock.

Concept note-4: -Gneiss is a metamorphic rock formed by changing schist, granite, or volcanic rocks through intense heat and pressure. Gneiss is foliated, which means that it has layers of lighter and darker minerals. These layers are of different densities and come about as a result of the intense pressure used to form gneiss.

Concept note-5: -Migmatite is a gneiss consisting of two or more distinct rock types, one of which has the appearance of an ordinary gneiss (the mesosome), and another of which has the appearance of an intrusive rock such pegmatite, aplite, or granite the (leucosome).
