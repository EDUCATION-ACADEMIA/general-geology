Which of the following is most likely to contribute to chemical weathering?

(A) trees

(B) wind

(C) ice

(D) ** acid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -High temperatures and greater rainfall increase the rate of chemical weathering. 2. Rocks in tropical regions exposed to abundant rainfall and hot temperatures weather much faster than similar rocks residing in cold, dry regions.

Concept note-2: -When rocks are exposed to acidic substances the acids react with minerals inside the rocks, weakening the structure of the rock and causing it to break down more quickly. For example, when carbon dioxide and water mix to form carbonic acid, this acid can get inside of rocks and cause chemical weathering.

Concept note-3: -Water, ice, acids, salts, plants, animals, and changes in temperature are all agents of weathering.
