Soil is the result of which of the following processes?

(A) Erosion by glaciers

(B) ** Weathering and biologic activity

(C) Erosion by running water

(D) Deposition and human activity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chemical weathering by biological action is also known as biological weathering. The materials left over after the rock breaks down combine with organic material to create soil. Many of Earthâ€™s landforms and landscapes are the result of weathering processes combined with erosion and re-deposition.

Concept note-2: -Soil forms through the mechanical and chemical weathering of rocks and sediments, and the accumulation and decay of organic matter. The factors that affect the nature of soil and the rate of its formation include: Climate, especially average temperature and precipitation amounts, and the consequent types of vegetation.

Concept note-3: -Biological weathering occurs when plants break up rocks with roots or root exudates. The process is slow, but may strongly influence landscape formation.

Concept note-4: -Biological weathering is caused by the movements of plants and animals. For example, a rabbit can burrow into a crack in a rock making it bigger and eventually splitting the rock, or a plant may grow in a crack in a rock and, as its roots grow, cause the crack to widen.

Concept note-5: -Trees put down roots through joints or cracks in the rock in order to find moisture. As the tree grows, the roots gradually prize the rock apart. Many animals, such as these Piddock shells, bore into rocks for protection either by scraping away the grains or secreting acid to dissolve the rock.
