When scientists are trying to compare things to see which geological artifacts are older or younger rather than find the actual age of something, this is called ____

(A) ** Relative aging

(B) Geologica aging

(C) approximate aging

(D) absolute aging

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Relative dating is used to arrange geological events, and the rocks they leave behind, in a sequence. The method of reading the order is called stratigraphy (layers of rock are called strata ). Relative dating does not provide actual numerical dates for the rocks.

Concept note-2: -To establish the age of a rock or a fossil, researchers use some type of clock to determine the date it was formed. Geologists commonly use radiometric dating methods, based on the natural radioactive decay of certain elements such as potassium and carbon, as reliable clocks to date ancient events.

Concept note-3: -Superposition. This principle states that a sequence of rocks in their original orientation will have the oldest rock on the bottom and the youngest rock on the top.

Concept note-4: -Stratigraphy is the study of the order of the layers of rocks and where they fit in the geological timescale. This method is most effective for studying sedimentary rocks. Cross dating is a method of using fossils to determine the relative age of a rock.

Concept note-5: -The numerical ages of rocks in the Geologic Time Scale are determined by radiometric dating, which makes use of a process called radioactive decay â€“ the same process that goes on inside a nuclear reactor to produce heat to make electricity. Radiometric dating works because radioactive elements decay at a known rate.
