Which would a scientist most likely study to develop a better understanding of the climate during a specific period in Earth’s history?

(A) fossils

(B) ** ice cores

(C) igneous rocks

(D) sedimentary rock

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ice cores provide direct information about how greenhouse gas concentrations have changed in the past, and they also provide direct evidence that the climate can change abruptly under some circumstances.

Concept note-2: -Scientists study Earth’s climate and how it changes in a variety of different ways, using satellite, instrumental, historical, and environmental records. One challenge of using satellite and instrumental data is that their lifespans have been rather short when compared to Earth’s life.

Concept note-3: -When scientists focus on climate from before the past 100-150 years, they use records from physical, chemical, and biological materials preserved within the geologic record. Organisms (such as diatoms, forams, and coral) can serve as useful climate proxies.
