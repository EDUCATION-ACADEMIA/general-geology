What is a river mouth?

(A) The place where a river starts

(B) ** Where the river flows into the sea

(C) Where two river channels meet

(D) The land next to the river channel

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A delta is formed where a river empties into a larger body of water and deposits large amounts of alluvium. The biggest delta in the world is the Ganges Delta in Bangladesh and India.

Concept note-2: -An estuary is an area where a freshwater river or stream meets the ocean. In estuaries, the salty ocean mixes with a freshwater river, resulting in brackish water. Brackish water is somewhat salty, but not as salty as the ocean. An estuary may also be called a bay, lagoon, sound, or slough.

Concept note-3: -Estuaries: Where the River Meets the Sea.

Concept note-4: -A river mouth is where the river ends, but an estuary is typically a transition area that exists before a river reaches its final destination at a saltwater location. Estuaries are somewhat closed-off bodies of water where rivers and saltwater meet.
