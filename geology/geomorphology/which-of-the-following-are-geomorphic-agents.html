Which of the following are geomorphic agents?

(A) Running water, groundwater, continent building forces, earthquakes, volcanism

(B) mountain building forces, continent building forces, earthquakes, volcanism

(C) mountain building forces, the wind, waves, and currents

(D) ** Running water, groundwater, glaciers, the wind, waves, and currents

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An agent is a mobile medium (like running water, moving ice masses, wind, waves and currents etc.) which removes, transports and deposits earth materials. Running water, groundwater, glaciers, wind, waves and currents, etc., can be called geomorphic agents.

Concept note-2: -Running water is the most important geomorphic agent, actively changing landforms by eroding and transferring sediments. It shapes the earthâ€™s surface by attrition, vertical and lateral erosion, and deposition. There are two components of running water. One is an overland flow on the general land surface as a sheet.

Concept note-3: -The evolution of landforms depends on geomorphic agents i.e., running water, groundwater, glaciers, waves and winds. These agents reduce landmasses through erosion and also develop some landforms through deposition. Both erosion and deposition change the surface of the earth.

Concept note-4: -There are many sub disciplines in geomorphology including tectonic, fluvial, storm, aeolian, floodplain, glacial, groundwater, climate, tsunami, and many others. These sub disciplines are mainly driven by distinctions in the mechanics and dynamics involved in the processes.

Concept note-5: -A geomorphic agent is a mobile medium that transports, removes, and deposits earth materials. Running water, glaciers, wind, waves, ocean currents, groundwater, and other geomorphic agents are examples.
