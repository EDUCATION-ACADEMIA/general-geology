The place that an earthquake starts is____

(A) ** the focus

(B) the epicenter

(C) rock

(D) boundary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The point where an earthquake originates is called the seismic focus. It is also known as hypocenter.

Concept note-2: -The spot underground where the rock first breaks is called the focus, or hypocenter of the earthquake. The place right above the focus (at the ground surface) is called the epicenter of the earthquake. Cutaway view of the reverse fault. The earthquake starts at the focus, with slip continuing along the fault.

Concept note-3: -The point where the earthquake originates is called the seismic focus. The point on the Earthâ€™s surface vertically above the focus is called its epicentre.

Concept note-4: -Shallow-focus earthquakes occur more often than deeper ones; about 75% of the total energy released from earthquakes is from shallow-focus ones. Unfortunately, the shallower an earthquake, the more damage it can produce at the surface; intermediate and deep-focus earthquakes are rarely destructive.
