State animal of Kerala

(A) ** Elephant

(B) Tiger

(C) Cow

(D) Lion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The state animal of Kerala is Asian Elephant (also known as the Indian Elephant) is a huge land animal that lives in India, Malaysia, Sumatra, and Sri Lanka . This elephant is used extensively for labor; very few are left in the wild. Their life span is up to 70 years.

Concept note-2: -As the State Animal, the elephant is featured on the emblem of the Government of Kerala state, taken from the Royal Arms of both Travancore and Cochin. It is believed that an elephant that has been captured in the wild, and tamed, will never be accepted by other wild elephants.

Concept note-3: -Ans. Jharkhand, Karnataka and Kerala are the three states whose State animal is the Elephant.
