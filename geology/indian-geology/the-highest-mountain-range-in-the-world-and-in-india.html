The highest mountain range in the world and in India

(A) Mt. Everest

(B) ** Himalayan Mountains

(C) Hindu Kush Mountains

(D) Denali

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Himalaya is the world’s highest mountain range home to all fourteen peaks over 8, 000m including Everest and K2 . Himalaya means “abode of snow” with an estimated 15, 000 glaciers throughout the range.

Concept note-2: -The Great Himalayas or Greater Himalayas or Himadri is the highest mountain range of the Himalayan Range. The world’s highest peak, Mount Everest, as well as other “near−highest” peaks, such as Kangchenjunga, Lhotse, and Nanga Parbat, are part of the Greater Himalayas range.

Concept note-3: -The Himalayas holds the record for the world’s tallest mountain range. The highest range of the Himalayas is called Mount Everest and is about 8, 848 metre high above mean sea level.

Concept note-4: -2. K2, Karakoram, Pakistan/China – 8611m. The second highest mountain in the world is K2. The mountain took its name from the notation used by the Great Trigonometrical Survey of British India.
