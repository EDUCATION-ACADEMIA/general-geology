How can “biomimicry” be described?

(A) when a form of life is cloned

(B) when scientists attempt to adopt animal qualities

(C) when biologists study to become fossil hunters

(D) ** when an engineer attempts to take a process in nature and replicate it in a new form of technology

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Biomimicry engineering is the practice of creating designs based on structures and sequences from the Earth’s natural world, including its plants, animals and geographies.

Concept note-2: -Biomimicry can be achieved at different levels: mimicking nature form or function, mimicking natural processes, and mimicking natural systems. Mimicking form or function are the most common biomimetics seen in biomedical research.

Concept note-3: -This is called biomimicry: but really, what is it? Biomimicry-or biomimetics-is the examination of nature, its systems, its procedures, processes and elements to then take inspiration for new inventions that help humans solve problems in which they are facing.

Concept note-4: -DEFINE. Clearly articulate the impact you want your design to have in the world (i.e. the challenge you want to solve) and the criteria and constraints that will determine success. BIOLOGIZE. Analyze the essential functions and context your design solution must address. DISCOVER. ABSTRACT. EMULATE. EVALUATE.
