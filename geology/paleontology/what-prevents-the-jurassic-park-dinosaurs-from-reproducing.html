What prevents the jurassic park dinosaurs from reproducing?

(A) ** They’re all female

(B) They’re all male

(C) They just don’t like each other

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The research scientists retrieved dinosaur DNA from biting insects that have been preserved within ancient amber. Dr. Wu explains that to prevent breeding, their dinosaurs are sterilized and engineered to be female.

Concept note-2: -That’s the illusion.” In the beginning of the franchise, we’re told that all the dinosaurs in Jurassic Park are female. This is because, as Wu says, “all vertebrate embryos are inherently female, anyway. They just require an extra hormone given at the right developmental stage to make them male.

Concept note-3: -Dr. Wu explains that, to prevent breeding, their dinosaurs are sterilized and all of them are engineered to be female.

Concept note-4: -They reproduce through parthenogenesis Parthenogenesis is a form of asexual reproduction in which the egg develops into a new individual without fertilization from a second parent. It’s actually fairly common in certain types of animals.

Concept note-5: -In the movie, scientists managed to clone dinosaurs by finding dinosaur blood within mosquitoes that had been fossilized in amber. They then used the DNA in the blood to clone the dinosaurs for their park. In real life, alas, even the very premise of Jurassic Park turns out to be false.
