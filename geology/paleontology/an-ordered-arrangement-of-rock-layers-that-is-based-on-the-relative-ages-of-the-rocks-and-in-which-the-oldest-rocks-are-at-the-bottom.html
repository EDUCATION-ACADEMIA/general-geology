An ordered arrangement of rock layers that is based on the relative ages of the rocks and in which the oldest rocks are at the bottom

(A) Half- life

(B) Paleontology

(C) ** Geologic column

(D) Relative age

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -geologic column: an ordered arrangement of rock layers that is based on the relative ages of the rocks and in which the oldest rocks are at the bottom. Evidence of changing conditions is recorded in the rock layers of Earthâ€™s crust. Geologic time scale shows the development of Earth and of life on Earth.

Concept note-2: -A geologic column is an ordered arrangement of rock layers based on the relative ages of the rocks, with the oldest rocks at the bottom. Scientists develop geologic columns to piece together the geologic record of a large area.

Concept note-3: -law of superposition, a major principle of stratigraphy stating that within a sequence of layers of sedimentary rock, the oldest layer is at the base and that the layers are progressively younger with ascending order in the sequence.

Concept note-4: -The layers in order are Cambrian, Ordovician, Silurian, Devonian, Carboniferous, (Mississippian and Pennsylvanian), Permian, Triassic, Jurassic, Cretaceous, Tertiary, and Quaternary. Sedimentary rock is the type of rock in which fossils are most likely to form.

Concept note-5: -The relative age of a rock is its age in comparison with other rocks. If you know the relative ages of two rock layers, you know which is older and which is younger, but you do not know how old the layers are in years.
