Scientists have found fossil evidence that one group of dinosaurs had feathers and evolved into today’s birds. Which of the following supports this evolutionary connection?

(A) ** Recent fossil discoveries suggest that some dinosaurs had light, hollow bones.

(B) All dinosaurs were warm-blooded endotherms.

(C) Recent fossil discoveries suggest that some dinosaurs had heavy, solid bones.

(D) Most dinosaurs were cold-blooded ectotherms

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 2011, samples of amber were discovered to contain preserved feathers from 75 to 80 million years ago during the Cretaceous era, with evidence that they were from both dinosaurs and birds. Initial analysis suggests that some of the feathers were used for insulation, and not flight.

Concept note-2: -Darwin’s friend Thomas Huxley noticed similarities between Archaeopteryx and a fossil dinosaur called Compsognathus. Based on these similarities, he proposed that birds were related to dinosaurs.

Concept note-3: -In fact, most dinosaurs with strong evidence of feathers come from within a very select group of theropods known as the Coelurosauria. This includes not only tyrannosaurs and birds, but also the ornithomimosaurs, therizinosaurs and compsognathids.

Concept note-4: -Archaeopteryx was first recognised as a species in the 1860s. It was immediately seized on as evidence for Darwin’s theory of evolution, because it appeared to be a bird with dinosaur-like traits. It had wings and feathers, but teeth instead of a beak.
