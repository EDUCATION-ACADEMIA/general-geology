What is a divergent tectonic plate

(A) ** A plate that moves away from another

(B) A plate that moves toward anohter

(C) A plate that slides past another

(D) Watersheep

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A divergent boundary occurs when two tectonic plates move away from each other. Along these boundaries, earthquakes are common and magma (molten rock) rises from the Earthâ€™s mantle to the surface, solidifying to create new oceanic crust. The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is an example of divergent plate boundaries.

Concept note-2: -Divergent (Spreading):This is where two plates move away from each other. Molten rock from the mantle erupts along the opening, forming new crust. The earthquakes that occur along these zones, called spreading centers, are relatively small.

Concept note-3: -At a divergent boundary the plates move apart from each other as fresh magma rises to the surface to create new crustal material.

Concept note-4: -False, because the correct statement is: There are three types of divergent plate boundaries, namely continental-continental, oceanic-continental, and oceanic-oceanic.

Concept note-5: -There are two types of divergent boundaries, categorized by where they occur: continental rift zones and mid-ocean ridges.
