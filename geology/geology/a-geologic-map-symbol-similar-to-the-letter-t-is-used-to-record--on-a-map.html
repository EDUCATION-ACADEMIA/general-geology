A geologic map symbol similar to the letter “T” is used to record ____ on a map

(A) Formation boundaries

(B) the location of fossils

(C) the orientation of a fold axis

(D) ** strike and dip

(E) the orientation of joints

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On geologic maps, strike and dip can be represented by a T symbol with a number next to it. The longer line represents strike, and is in the same orientation as the strike angle. Dip is represented by the shorter line, which is perpendicular to the strike line in the downhill direction.

Concept note-2: -The strike and dip symbol is a T with the long horizontal bar representing the strike direction, and the small tick mark indicating the dip direction. The dip angle is written next to the tick mark. On a map, geologists use a strike and dip symbol to represent inclined beds (Figure 3).

Concept note-3: -Dip is the angle at which a planar feature is inclined to the horizontal plane; it is measured in a vertical plane perpendicular to the strike of the feature.

Concept note-4: -strike, in geology, direction of the line formed by the intersection of a fault, bed, or other planar feature and a horizontal plane. Strike indicates the attitude or position of linear structural features such as faults, beds, joints, and folds.
