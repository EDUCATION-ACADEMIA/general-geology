Concept note-2: -This process is known as evidence-based argumentation. The figure below explains the three components of a scientific argument-the claim (or the explanation), the evidence (or the observations), and the rationale (or the reasoning).

Concept note-3: -Arguments can be divided into four general components: claim, reason, support, and warrant. Claims are statements about what is true or good or about what should be done or believed. Claims are potentially arguable.

Concept note-4: -Toulmin identifies the three essential parts of any argument as the claim; the data (also called grounds or evidence), which support the claim; and the warrant. The warrant is the assumption on which the claim and the evidence depend.
