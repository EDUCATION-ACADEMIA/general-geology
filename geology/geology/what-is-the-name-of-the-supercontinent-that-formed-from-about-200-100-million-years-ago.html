What is the name of the supercontinent that formed from about 200-100 million years ago?

(A) ** Pangaea

(B) Laurasia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -About 200 million years ago, all the continents on Earth were actually one huge “supercontinent” surrounded by one enormous ocean. This gigantic continent, called Pangaea, slowly broke apart and spread out to form the continents we know today.

Concept note-2: -From about 300-200 million years ago (late Paleozoic Era until the very late Triassic), the continent we now know as North America was contiguous with Africa, South America, and Europe. They all existed as a single continent called Pangea.

Concept note-3: -About 200 million years ago Pangaea broke into two new continents Laurasia and Gondwanaland. Laurasia was made of the present day continents of North America (Greenland), Europe, and Asia. Gondwanaland was made of the present day continents of Antarctica, Australia, South America.

Concept note-4: -Pangea, also spelled Pangaea, in early geologic time, a supercontinent that incorporated almost all the landmasses on Earth.

Concept note-5: -Supercontinents have aggregated and dispersed several times during geologic history, although geologic record of supercontinent cycles is only well documented for the last two cycles: Gondwana–Pangea and Rodinia.
