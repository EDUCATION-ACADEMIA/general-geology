What is a sudden and violent shifting in Earth’s crust and may occur when two plates slide past each other?

(A) ** Earthquake

(B) Volcano

(C) Tsunami

(D) Tornado

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When tectonic plates move, it also causes movements at the faults. An earthquake is the sudden movement of Earth’s crust at a fault line. The location where an earthquake begins is called the epicenter. An earthquake’s most intense shaking is often felt near the epicenter.

Concept note-2: -Learn about who is most at risk for emotional distress from earthquakes and where to find disaster-related resources. An earthquake is the shifting of the Earth’s plates, which results in a sudden shaking of the ground that can last for a few seconds to a few minutes.

Concept note-3: -Convergent (Colliding): This occurs when plates move towards each other and collide.

Concept note-4: -When the force of movement finally overcomes the friction, sections of the crust suddenly break or become displaced, releasing the pent-up pressure in the form of seismic waves. This is a naturally occurring earthquake, sometimes referred to as a tectonic earthquake.

Concept note-5: -Earthquakes are the result of sudden movement along faults within the Earth. The movement releases stored-up ‘elastic strain’ energy in the form of seismic waves, which propagate through the Earth and cause the ground surface to shake.
