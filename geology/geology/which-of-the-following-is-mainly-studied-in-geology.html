Which of the following is mainly studied in geology?

(A) how the Earth has changed over time

(B) ** the solid earth, including the materials that make up the planet and the processes that shape the planet

(C) the interaction between humans and the geological environment

(D) the interaction between the Earth and the rest of the solar system

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Geology is the study of the lithosphere, or Earth’s surface, including the crust and rocks. It includes the physical characteristics and processes that occur in the lithosphere as well as how they are affected by geothermal energy.

Concept note-2: -Geology is the study of the solid Earth. Geologists study how rocks and minerals form. The way mountains rise up is part of geology.

Concept note-3: -As a geology student, you will learn about a range of topics such as the origins and development of landscapes, earthquakes, volcanoes, sustainability, glaciers, water composition and processes, floods, groundwater flow, the interaction between humans and the planet, and dinosaur evolution.

Concept note-4: -Geology involves studying the materials that make up the earth, the features and structures found on Earth as well as the processes that act upon them. Geology also deals with the study of the history of all life that’s ever lived on or is living on the earth now.

Concept note-5: -Geology is an Earth science concerned with the solid Earth, the rocks of which it is composed, and the processes by which they change over time. Geology can also include the study of the solid features of any terrestrial planet or natural satellite such as Mars or the Moon.
