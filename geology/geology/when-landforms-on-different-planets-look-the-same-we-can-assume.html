When landforms on different planets look the same, we can assume____

(A) they were formed by different processes.

(B) ** they were formed by the same geologic processes

(C) they are not related

(D) they may or may not be somewhat close to each other in location.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When landforms on different rocky planets look similar, it is evidence that they may have been formed by the same geologic process. Scientists can use models to test their ideas and get evidence about processes in the natural world that are difficult to share.

Concept note-2: -Landforms can provide evidence about the past because they remain after the geologic process that formed them stop happening. Models perfectly represent the natural processes being investigated.

Concept note-3: -Geologic Processes These include: diagenesis, Earthquakes, erosion, glaciation, hydrothermal processes, isostacy, land subsidence, liquefaction, metamorphism, sediment transport, sedimentation, tectonic processes, volcanic activity, Earth tides, deformation, soil formation, magnetic storms, and mass wasting.

Concept note-4: -Geological processes are events that occur on a geological timescale ranging between millions of centuries, hundreds of meters, and thousands of kilometers. Compare this to the everyday models from physics and engineering operated at laboratory units and the scale of human lifetime.
