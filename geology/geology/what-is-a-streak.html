What is a streak?

(A) ** the color of a mineral when it is grounded in a powder

(B) the color of a mineral when it is in water

(C) the color of a mineral when it metamorphosed

(D) the color of a mineral when it dances

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -streak, the colour of a mineral in its powdered form. It is usually obtained by rubbing the mineral on a hard, white surface, such as a tile of unglazed porcelain, so as to yield a line, or streak, of fine powder.

Concept note-2: -The color of a mineral in powdered form is called the mineral’s streak. To find a mineral’s streak, the mineral is rubbed against a piece of unglazed porcelain called a streak plate. The mark left on the streak plate is the streak.

Concept note-3: -Streak is simply the color of a mineral powder. Many minerals have a different color when powdered than they do in crystal or massive forms. The color may be entirely different, or it may be a different shade. Quite a few minerals give a powder streak that is lighter in color than the whole crystal or massive pieces.

Concept note-4: -The streak (also called “powder color") of a mineral is the color of the powder produced when it is dragged across an unweathered surface. This trail’s color generally is much more consistent than that of its parent mineral and therefore is an important mineral identification tool.

Concept note-5: -The color of a mineral’s powder is called its ‘streak. ‘ This term comes from the ‘streak test, ’ which is the method by which a mineral’s powder is examined. A sample of the mineral is swiped across a ‘streak plate’ so that the powder left behind can be examined.
