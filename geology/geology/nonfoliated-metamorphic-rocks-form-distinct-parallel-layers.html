Nonfoliated metamorphic rocks form distinct, parallel layers.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nonfoliated metamorphic rocks form distinct, parallel layers. Studying a rock’s texture can help scientists determine which type of rock it is. A volcanic eruption creates an intrusive igneous rock.

Concept note-2: -A metamorphic rock with no banding is non foliated. This simply means that the bands, or layers, of minerals in the rock are not parallel to each other. Non foliated rocks can still have a layered or banded appearance, but the minerals are not arranged in flat, parallel layers.

Concept note-3: -Non-foliated rocks form when pressure is uniform, or near the surface where pressure is very low. They can also form when the parent rock consists of blocky minerals such as quartz and calcite, in which individual crystals do not align because they aren’t longer in any one dimension.

Concept note-4: -Metamorphic rocks are classified into two groups based on texture: foliated rocks and nonfoliated rocks. Foliated rocks contain parallel layers of flat and elongated minerals. Metamorphic rocks that have mineral grains with a random, interlocking texture are nonfoliated rocks.

Concept note-5: -Nonfoliated metamorphic rocks lack foliated texture because they often lack platy minerals such as micas. They commonly result from contact or regional metamorphism. Examples include marble, quartzite, greenstone, hornfel, and anthracite.
