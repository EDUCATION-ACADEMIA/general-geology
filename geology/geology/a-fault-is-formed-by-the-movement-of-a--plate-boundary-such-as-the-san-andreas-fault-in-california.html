A fault is formed by the movement of a ____ plate boundary, such as the San Andreas Fault in California.

(A) ** Transform

(B) Convergent

(C) Divergent

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The San Andreas Fault and Queen Charlotte Fault are transform plate boundaries developing where the Pacific Plate moves northward past the North American Plate. The San Andreas Fault is just one of several faults that accommodate the transform motion between the Pacific and North American plates.

Concept note-2: -The 1, 200-kilometer-long San Andreas fault zone is part of the boundary between the Pacific and North American plates, and thus is known as a transform fault. It connects the East Pacific Rise in the Gulf of California with the junction of the Mendocino fracture zone and the Cascade subduction zone to the north.

Concept note-3: -Tectonic plates grind past each other at transform fault boundaries. Neither production nor consumption of lithosphere occurs at these boundaries. For U.S. residents, the most famous example is the San Andreas fault in California.

Concept note-4: -The Northern Pacific plate is sliding laterally past the North American plate in a northerly direction, and hence the San Andreas is classified as a strike-slip fault.

Concept note-5: -The San Andreas Fault is an example of transform boundaries where plate material is neither created nor destroyed. In this case, two plates are moving past one another in opposite directions. This is referred to as transform motion and thus the San Andreas is referred to as a transform fault.
