The negative effect of deforestation is____

(A) ** soil erosion

(B) more habitat for flora and fauna

(C) more oxygen produces

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As a result, deforestation will probably mean the soil will become increasingly fragile, leaving the area more vulnerable to natural disasters such as landslides and floods.

Concept note-2: -The loss of trees and other vegetation can cause climate change, desertification, soil erosion, fewer crops, flooding, increased greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, and a host of problems for Indigenous people.

Concept note-3: -The major causes of soil erosion are namely, deforestation, overgrazing, forest fires and poor farming techniques. Hence the statement is correct.

Concept note-4: -reduced ability of the soil to store water and nutrients. exposure of subsoil, which often has poor physical and chemical properties. higher rates of runoff, shedding water and nutrients otherwise used for crop growth. loss of newly planted crops.

Concept note-5: -The loss of trees, which anchor the soil with their roots, causes widespread erosion throughout the tropics. Only a minority of areas have good soils, which after clearing are quickly washed away by the heavy rains.
