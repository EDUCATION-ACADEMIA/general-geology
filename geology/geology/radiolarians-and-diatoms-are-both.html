Radiolarians and diatoms are both:

(A) ** provide shells that form siliceous oozes.

(B) calcareous oozes in the deepest parts of the ocean.

(C) multicellular organisms.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Siliceous oozes are made up of the remains of diatoms, a microscopic phytoplankton, and radiolaria, a microscopic zooplankton. Diatoms are one of the most important primary producers in the ocean.

Concept note-2: -Siliceous marine organisms, such as diatoms and radiolarians, use silica to form skeletons through a process known as biomineralization.

Concept note-3: -Siliceous oozes are composed of opal (amorphous, hydrated silica) that forms the skeleton of various microorganisms, including diatoms, radiolarians, siliceous sponges, and silicoflagellates.
