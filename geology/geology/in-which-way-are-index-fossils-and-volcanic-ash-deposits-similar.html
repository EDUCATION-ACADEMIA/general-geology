In which way are index fossils and volcanic ash deposits similar?

(A) Both can usually be dated with radiocarbon

(B) Both normally occur in nonsedimentary rocks

(C) Both strongly resist chemical weathering

(D) ** Both often serve as geologic time markers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Index fossils are used to define geological periods. These fossils can be defined as “commonly found, widely distributed fossils that are limited in time span.” If one finds an index fossil in a given layer, then one has bounds on the age of the layer. Using index fossils, the geological periods are defined.

Concept note-2: -Volcanic ash from a single eruption can be DATED, which means you can get a very reliable real age from each ash layer (representing a single eruption). And an ash layer typically covers the surface of the ground at the time of the eruption, so you also get a reliable idea of what the terrain looked like at the time.

Concept note-3: -Some of the most common index fossils are ammonites, trilobites, and graptolites. Index fossils are vital components in relative dating-a method of determining the order of geological events without knowing their numerical dates.

Concept note-4: -Index fossils are fossils that are widespread but only existed for a short period of time. Index fossils help scientists to find the relative age of a rock layer and match it up with other rock layers. Living fossils are organisms that haven’t changed much in millions of years and are still alive today.
