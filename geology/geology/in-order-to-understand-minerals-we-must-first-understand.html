In order to understand minerals, we must first understand____

(A) Rocks

(B) ** Atoms

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In order to understand minerals we must have some basic understanding of atoms-what they are and how they interact with one another to form minerals. A particular mineral cannot be formed unless the chemical ingredients necessary to make the mineral are present.

Concept note-2: -Others are made when hot gases or molten rock cool or when heat or pressure change pre-existing minerals. Minerals start as atoms moving randomly in a fluid. When the fluid cools, the atoms link together in regular shapes and form a crystal lattice.

Concept note-3: -In geology, the classic definition of a mineral is: 1) naturally occurring, 2) inorganic, 3) solid at room temperature, 4) regular crystal structure, and 5) defined chemical composition. Some natural substances technically should not be considered minerals, but are included by exception.

Concept note-4: -The atoms in a mineral are arranged in an organised atomic structure. They connect together to form molecules, and the molecules stack together in a regular pattern to form a crystal. The shape of a crystal depends on the way the molecules are stacked up inside it.

Concept note-5: -This arrangement of atoms determines a mineral type. All minerals have a chemical formula, which is an analysis of the types and amounts of elements present in a mineral.
