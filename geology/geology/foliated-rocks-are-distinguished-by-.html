Foliated rocks are distinguished by ____

(A) ** mineral bands or planes

(B) no mineral bands or planes

(C) size of crystals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Correct! Foliated rocks are defined by the parallel alignment of mineral grains that are mostly thin and planar such as micas or in some cases hornblende.

Concept note-2: -A foliated, high-grade metamorphic rock distinguished by its strong preferred orientation, or banding, of light and dark minerals. The strong preferred orientation of mineral grains in a metamorphic rock, which is seen as a banding or separation of light and dark minerals.

Concept note-3: -Foliated rocks have platy or elongate minerals aligned in roughly parallel planes or in wavy bands or planes.

Concept note-4: -Foliated Metamorphic Rocks: Some kinds of metamorphic rocks–granite gneiss and biotite schist are two examples–are strongly banded or foliated. (Foliated means the parallel arrangement of certain mineral grains that gives the rock a striped appearance.)

Concept note-5: -foliation, planar arrangement of structural or textural features in any rock type but particularly that resulting from the alignment of constituent mineral grains of a metamorphic rock of the regional variety along straight or wavy planes.
