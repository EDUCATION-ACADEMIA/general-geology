What 2 things are needed to measure the intensity of earthquakes?

(A) Seismograph and Key Scale

(B) ** Seismograph and Richter Scale

(C) Richter Scale and Bore Gauge

(D) Polygraph and Richter Scale

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The intensity of earthquake shaking at any location is determined not only by the magnitude of the earthquake and its distance, but also by the type of underlying rock or unconsolidated materials.

Concept note-2: -In 1935, Charles Richter developed the Richter scale, which measures the amount of ground shaking and energy released from an earthquake.

Concept note-3: -The strength, size and impact of an earthquake are typically described using two types of measurement: magnitude and intensity scales. Although often confused, they each measure different characteristics of an earthquake.

Concept note-4: -A seismograph is the primary earthquake measuring instrument. The seismograph produces a digital graphic recording of the ground motion caused by the seismic waves. The digital recording is called a seismogram. A network of worldwide seismographs detects and measures the strength and duration of the earthquake’s waves.

Concept note-5: -How do they work? A seismometer is the internal part of the seismograph, which may be a pendulum or a mass mounted on a spring; however, it is often used synonymously with “seismograph". Seismographs are instruments used to record the motion of the ground during an earthquake.
