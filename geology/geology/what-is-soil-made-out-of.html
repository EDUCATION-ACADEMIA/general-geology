What is soil made out of?

(A) Dirt

(B) Weathered Rock

(C) Organic Material

(D) ** Weathered Rock and Organic Material

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -3 Humus Humus is dark-colored organic matter made of pieces of decaying plants and animals Worms and insects mix the humus with the fragments of rock. The O horizon is filled with organic matter (leaf litter). The A horizon, known as topsoil, it is the most fully evolved layer in a soil profile.

Concept note-2: -Soil is a mixture of four materials: weathered rock particles, organic matter, water, and air. Weathered rock particles are the main ingredient of soil. Soils differ, depending on what types of rock the rock particles came from-for example, granite or limestone.

Concept note-3: -Soil is the thin layer of material covering the earthâ€™s surface and is formed from the weathering of rocks. It is made up mainly of mineral particles, organic materials, air, water and living organisms-all of which interact slowly yet constantly.

Concept note-4: -The Black soil is also known as regur soil and is formed by the weathering of Igneous rocks that form the Deccan plateau. Black soil is developed over Deccan lava granite rocks under semi-arid conditions.

Concept note-5: -Soil consists of weathered rocks, decomposed organic material from dead plants, animals, and bacteria. Soils are often found in layers, with each having a different chemical composition and texture.
