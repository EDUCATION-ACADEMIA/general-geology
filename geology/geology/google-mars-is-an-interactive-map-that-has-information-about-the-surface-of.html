Google Mars is an interactive map that has information about the surface of:

(A) Mercury

(B) ** Mars

(C) Earth

(D) Venus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Can I see the Mars data using the Google Earth client? Yes, you can! Mars in Google Earth allows you to fly to the red planet in a virtual 3D environment.

Concept note-2: -Its surface is rocky, with canyons, volcanoes, dry lake beds and craters all over it. Red dust covers most of its surface. Mars has clouds and wind just like Earth. Sometimes the wind blows the red dust into a dust storm.

Concept note-3: -Nearly 90% of Mars’ surface has been mapped by the high-resolution stereo camera on ESA’s Mars Express, which celebrates ten years since launch this June.

Concept note-4: -A near-global map of Mars, composed of nearly 51, 000 mapping strips mosaicked into 1, 764 tiles. How they did it-CRISM has made about 33, 000 high-resolution images over the years, and they’ve helped identify landing sites for rover missions, and also inform the future Mars Sample Return mission.
