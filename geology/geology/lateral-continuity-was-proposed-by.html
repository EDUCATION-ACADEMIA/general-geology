Lateral continuity was proposed by

(A) William Smith

(B) Alfred Wallace

(C) Charles Darwin

(D) ** Nicholas Steno

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Steno’s final principle is the “principle of lateral continuity, ” which says that sediment layers spread out until they reach an obstacle that keeps them from spreading further, the way soup spreads out in a bowl until it reaches the sides of the dish.

Concept note-2: -The principle of lateral continuity was invented by Niels Stensen. The lateral continuity principle was the third discovery of Niels.

Concept note-3: -Steno was the first to realize that the Earth’s crust contains a chronological history of geologic events and that the history may be deciphered by careful study of the strata and fossils. He rejected the idea that mountains grow like trees, proposing instead that they are formed by alterations of the Earth’s crust.

Concept note-4: -Nicholas Steno. Despite a relatively brief scientific career, Nicholas Steno’s work on the formation of rock layers and the fossils they contain was crucial to the development of modern geology. The principles he stated continue to be used today by geologists and paleontologists.

Concept note-5: -Once Steno proposed that fossils originally came from living things, he needed to explain how fossils ended up in the middle of rock. This led to Steno’s most significant contribution to geology. He suggested that all rock was originally formed as sediment was deposited from a fluid that covered the planet.
