True or False:A mineral has no definite chemical composition

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A mineral has one specific chemical composition, whereas a rock can be an aggregate of different minerals or mineraloids.

Concept note-2: -All minerals have a definite chemical composition. To understand chemical composition, we first need to understand element s and bonds. A chemical element is a pure substance, all the atoms are the same. The atoms that make up chemical elements are represented in the Periodic Table of Elements (Fig.

Concept note-3: -"Definite chemical composition” means that all occurrences of that mineral have a chemical composition that varies within a specific limited range. For example: the mineral halite (known as “rock salt” when it is mined) has a chemical composition of NaCl.

Concept note-4: -Detailed Solution The correct answer is All minerals are non-metallic. A naturally occuring substance that has a definite chemical composition is called Mineral. Minerals are not evenly distributed on Earth.

Concept note-5: -A mixture doesn’t have definite physical and chemical properties.
