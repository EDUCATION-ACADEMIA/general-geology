There are 3 types of rock. One is metamorphic rocks? What does that mean?

(A) the black part of the rock

(B) a dead rock

(C) a rock that changes colors

(D) ** the change of an existing rock

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Metamorphic rocks started out as some other type of rock, but have been substantially changed from their original igneous, sedimentary, or earlier metamorphic form. Metamorphic rocks form when rocks are subjected to high heat, high pressure, hot mineral-rich fluids or, more commonly, some combination of these factors.

Concept note-2: -Igneous rocks are formed from melted rock deep inside the Earth. Sedimentary rocks are formed from layers of sand, silt, dead plants, and animal skeletons. Metamorphic rocks formed from other rocks that are changed by heat and pressure underground.

Concept note-3: -Transformation of rocks from one form to another is called the rock cycle. For example, molten magma cools and becomes an igneous rock. It then breaks down into sediments and then forms a sedimentary rock under great pressure. The same rock can become a metamorphic rock when subjected to great heat and pressure.
