What is it called when two oceanic plates move apart and new crust is formed?

(A) crustal integrating

(B) ** sea-floor spreading

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Seafloor spreading is a geologic process in which tectonic plates-large slabs of Earth’s lithosphere-split apart from each other.

Concept note-2: -A divergent boundary occurs when two tectonic plates move away from each other. Along these boundaries, earthquakes are common and magma (molten rock) rises from the Earth’s mantle to the surface, solidifying to create new oceanic crust. The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is an example of divergent plate boundaries.

Concept note-3: -Plates move apart from each other along divergent boundaries. In the ocean basins these boundaries are the mid-ocean ridges, where new oceanic crust forms.

Concept note-4: -Seafloor spreading is a geologic process in which tectonic plates large slabs of Earth’s lithosphere split apart from each other. Seafloor spreading occurs along mid-ocean ridges (large mountain ranges rising from the ocean floor). The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is formed in the Atlantic ocean because of seafloor spreading.

Concept note-5: -Seafloor spreading is caused by divergent plates, which are a specific type of tectonic activity that results in plates that move away from each other. Divergent plates that are located underwater at mid-ocean ridges and volcanic activity present at these locations result in newly formed seafloor.
