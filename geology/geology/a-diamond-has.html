A diamond has
A
stronger bonds
B
weaker bonds
C
Either A or B
D
None of the above
Explanation: 

Detailed explanation-1: -In diamond, carbon atoms form four strong covalent bonds with other carbon atoms. These tetrahedra join up to form a large three dimensional lattice structure. There are no weak bonds in this structure, so it takes a lot more energy to break this material, giving rise to the high strength of diamond.

Detailed explanation-2: -In diamonds, each carbon atom is strongly bonded to four adjacent carbon atoms located at the apices of a tetrahedron (a three-sided pyramid). The four valence electrons of each carbon atom participate in the formation of very strong covalent bonds. These bonds have the same strength in all directions.

Detailed explanation-3: -Diamond is harder than graphite because each of its carbon atoms form four covalent bonds in a tetrahedral structure and also due to the presence of strong covalent bonds in it. A chemical bond involving the exchange of electron pairs between atoms is known as a covalent bond.

Detailed explanation-4: -Diamond is extremely strong owing to the bonded structure of its carbon atoms, where each carbon atom has four neighbors joined to it with sp3-hybrid covalent bonds.

Detailed explanation-5: -each carbon atom is joined to four other carbon atoms by covalent bonds. the carbon atoms have a regular lattice arrangement. there are no free electrons.
