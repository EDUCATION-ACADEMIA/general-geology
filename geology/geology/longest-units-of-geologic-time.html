longest units of geologic time

(A) ** eons

(B) epochs

(C) eras

(D) periods

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In formal usage, eons are the longest portions of geologic time (eras are the second-longest). Three eons are recognized: the Phanerozoic Eon (dating from the present back to the beginning of the Cambrian Period), the Proterozoic Eon, and the Archean Eon. Less formally, eon often refers to a span of one billion years.

Concept note-2: -Eon: Two or more eras compose an Eon. This is the largest division of time, lasting hundreds of millions of years.

Concept note-3: -eon = The largest unit of time. era = A unit of time shorter than an eon but longer than a period. period = A unit of time shorter than an era but longer than epoch. epoch = A unit of time shorter than a period but longer than an age.

Concept note-4: -Eras. Eons of geological time are subdivided into eras, which are the second-longest units of geological time. The Phanerozoic eon is divided into three eras: the Paleozoic, Mesozoic, and Cenozoic.
