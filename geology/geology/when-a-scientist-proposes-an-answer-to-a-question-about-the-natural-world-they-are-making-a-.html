When a scientist proposes an answer to a question about the natural world they are making a ____

(A) ** claim

(B) evidence

(C) reasoning

(D) hypothesis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -scientific hypothesis, an idea that proposes a tentative explanation about a phenomenon or a narrow set of phenomena observed in the natural world.

Concept note-2: -The proposed explanation or answer to a scientific question is called hypothesis.

Concept note-3: -Unlike art, philosophy, religion and other ways of knowing, science is based on empirical research. A scientist conducts this research to answer a question that she or he has about the natural world. Empirical research relies on systematic observation and experimentation, not on opinions and feelings.

Concept note-4: -The scientific method is the process of objectively establishing facts through testing and experimentation. The basic process involves making an observation, forming a hypothesis, making a prediction, conducting an experiment and finally analyzing the results.

Concept note-5: -Natural science is one of the branches of science concerned with the description, understanding and prediction of natural phenomena, based on empirical evidence from observation and experimentation.
