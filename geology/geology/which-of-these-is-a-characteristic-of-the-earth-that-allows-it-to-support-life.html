Which of these is a characteristic of the earth that allows it to support life?

(A) ** The right amount of gravity

(B) ** The right tilt and rotation

(C) ** Plenty of liquid water

(D) ** An atmosphere that protects it

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What makes the Earth habitable? It is the right distance from the Sun, it is protected from harmful solar radiation by its magnetic field, it is kept warm by an insulating atmosphere, and it has the right chemical ingredients for life, including water and carbon.

Concept note-2: -It is useful to categorize the requirements for life on Earth as four items: energy, carbon, liquid water, and various other elements. These are listed in Table 1 along with the occurrence of these factors in the Solar System (2).

Concept note-3: -The biosphere is made up of the parts of Earth where life exists. The biosphere extends from the deepest root systems of trees, to the dark environment of ocean trenches, to lush rainforests and high mountaintops.

Concept note-4: -The atmosphere protects life on earth by shielding it from incoming ultraviolet (UV) radiation, keeping the planet warm through insulation, and preventing extremes between day and night temperatures. The sun heats layers of the atmosphere causing it to convect driving air movement and weather patterns around the world.

Concept note-5: -It has both water and air, which are both indispensable for life. The presence of oxygen in the air in appropriate proportion supports life. These factors make the earth a unique planet.
