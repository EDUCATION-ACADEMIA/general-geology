What do you call the soil and rock overlaying a useful mineral deposit?

(A) Spoils

(B) ** Overburden

(C) Highwall

(D) Tailings

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Surface mining is a process whereby soil and rock overlying the mineral deposit are removed.

Concept note-2: -Overburden – soil and rock overlying a useful mineral deposit. Disturbed Land; mining accidents; health hazards; mine waste dumping; oil spills and blowouts; noise; ugliness; heat.

Concept note-3: -Overburden is the rock or soil layer that needs to be removed in order to access the ore being mined. Overburden is also referred to as spoil or waste. Overburdens are removed from surface mining and do not contain toxic components, unlike tailings, which is another type of underground mining waste.

Concept note-4: -2.2 Overburden Rock Overburden rock is the sedimentary rock above which compresses and consolidates the material below. In a petroleum system, the overburden rock overlies the source rock and contributes to its thermal maturation because of higher temperatures at greater depths.

Concept note-5: -Surface mining and Underground mining. Surface mining is a mining process where the rocks and soil overlying the mineral deposits are removed. It is often the preferred form of mining for most mining companies.
