A mineral is naturally occurring

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -â€œA mineral is a naturally occurring chemical compound, usually of crystalline form and abiogenic in origin. A mineral has one specific chemical composition, whereas a rock can be an aggregate of different minerals or mineraloids.

Concept note-2: -A mineral is a naturally occurring inorganic element or compound having an orderly internal structure and characteristic chemical composition, crystal form, and physical properties. Common minerals include quartz, feldspar, mica, amphibole, olivine, and calcite.

Concept note-3: -Minerals are naturally occurring substances which have certain physical properties and a definite chemical composition.

Concept note-4: -A mineral is a naturally occurring, inorganic solid with a definite chemical composition and a crystalline structure. A rock is an aggregate of one or more minerals. (A rock may also include organic remains.)

Concept note-5: -More than 4, 000 naturally occurring minerals-inorganic solids that have a characteristic chemical composition and specific crystal structure-have been found on Earth. They are formed of simple molecules or individual elements arranged in repeating chains, sheets, or three-dimensional arrays.
