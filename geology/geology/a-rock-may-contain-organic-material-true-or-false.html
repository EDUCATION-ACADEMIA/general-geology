A rock may contain organic material? True or False?

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Organic-rich sedimentary rocks are a specific type of sedimentary rock that contains significant amounts (>3%) of organic carbon. The most common types include coal, lignite, oil shale, or black shale.

Concept note-2: -The geological materials in rocks may be inorganic, but they can also include organic materials such as the partially decomposed plant matter preserved in coal. A rock can be composed of only one type of geological material or mineral, but many are composed of several types.

Concept note-3: -Organic sedimentary rocks are those containing large quantities of organic molecules. Organic molecules contain carbon, but in this context we are referring specifically to molecules with carbon-hydrogen bonds, such as materials from the soft tissues of plants and animals.

Concept note-4: -Rock is a solid mixture of crystals of one or more minerals, or organic matter.
