Refer to the following parts of the earth; “Outermost and thinnest zone of the earth that lies under the continents and ocean."

(A) Core

(B) ** Crust

(C) Mantle

(D) Asthenosphere

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The lithosphere is the solid, outer part of Earth. The lithosphere includes the brittle upper portion of the mantle and the crust, the outermost layers of Earth’s structure. It is bounded by the atmosphere above and the asthenosphere (another part of the upper mantle) below.

Concept note-2: -The Earth’s crust is the outer layer of the Earth and is about 5 to 25 miles thick depending on its location. It is the thinnest layer, only about 3-5 miles thick under the oceans which is called the oceanic crust.

Concept note-3: -Oceanic Crust is the thinnest layer of earth.

Concept note-4: -“Crust” describes the outermost shell of a terrestrial planet. Our planet’s thin, 40-kilometer (25-mile) deep crust-just 1% of Earth’s mass-contains all known life in the universe. Earth has three layers: the crust, the mantle, and the core. The crust is made of solid rocks and minerals.

Concept note-5: -The crust is made of rock. This layer is thinner under the oceans and much thicker in mountain ranges. There are two kinds of crust. Oceanic crust is thinner and denser than continental crust.
