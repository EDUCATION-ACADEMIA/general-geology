Which of these is an acceptable claim?

(A) ** The geologic process that created the channel on Mars was flowing water.

(B) water

(C) It was water

(D) The geologic process that created the channel on Mars was flowing lava.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The most likely geologic process to form the channel on Mars is continental drift, the same process that forms modern channels like the mid-Atlantic channel or the African Rift Valley.

Concept note-2: -We have considered satellite and aerial images of Earth showing landforms created by flowing lava and flowing water to see if these landforms were similar to the channel on Mars. These images gave us some evidence about what geologic process might have formed the channel.

Concept note-3: -They are then presented with new information: conglomerate rock was found near the base of the channel on Mars. This evidence strongly supports the claim that the channel was formed by flowing water.

Concept note-4: -Recent and continuing missions to Mars are showing that the Red Planet may be more geologically active than previously thought. Volcanoes and erosion by running water have shaped the surface. And evidence is growing that fluvial and possibly volcanic processes have been active in the very recent past.

Using an imaging spectrometer on MRO, researchers detected signatures of hydrated minerals on slopes where mysterious streaks are seen on the Red Planet.
