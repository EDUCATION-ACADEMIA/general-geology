The moving of sediments from place to place is known as what?

(A) sediment movement

(B) ** erosion

(C) evolution

(D) physical weathering

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The brown color indicates that bits of rock and soil are suspended in the fluid (air or water) and being transported from one place to another. This transported material is called sediment. Physical Erosion.

Concept note-2: -Sediment moves from one place to another through the process of erosion. Erosion is the removal and transportation of rock or soil. Erosion can move sediment through water, ice, or wind. Water can wash sediment, such as gravel or pebbles, down from a creek, into a river, and eventually to that riverâ€™s delta.

Concept note-3: -Erosion is the process of carrying away or displacement of sediment by the action of wind, water, gravity, or ice (Smith & Smith 1998). The process of deposition of sediment from a state of suspension or solution in a fluid is called sedimentation (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sediment).

Concept note-4: -The main forms of erosion are: surface erosion. fluvial erosion. mass-movement erosion.

Concept note-5: -This transport-and-deposition process of sediment is known as erosion. The act of breaking down monolithic material into smaller pieces is known as weathering. Weathering and erosion together are the two processes responsible for the movement of sediment across the globe.
