A mass extinction is the

(A) ** dying out of existing species

(B) evolution of many new species

(C) evolution of complex life-forms

(D) dying of a few species

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A mass extinction event is when species vanish much faster than they are replaced. This is usually defined as about 75% of the world’s species being lost in a short period of geological time-less than 2.8 million years.

Concept note-2: -A mass extinction is a short period of geological time in which a high percentage of biodiversity, or distinct species-bacteria, fungi, plants, mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish, invertebrates-dies out.

Concept note-3: -In addition to widespread species loss, mass extinctions change the trajectory of evolution by restructuring ecosystems, altering the dominant types of functional ecological groups, and affecting patterns of morphological evolution (3–6).

Concept note-4: -So the demise of dinosaurs like T. rex and Triceratops some 65 million years ago wouldn’t be especially noteworthy-except for the fact that around 50 percent of all plants and animals alive at the same time also died out in what scientists call a mass extinction.

Concept note-5: -The earliest known mass extinction, the Ordovician Extinction, took place at a time when most of the life on Earth lived in its seas. Its major casualties were marine invertebrates including brachiopods, trilobites, bivalves and corals; many species from each of these groups went extinct during this time.
