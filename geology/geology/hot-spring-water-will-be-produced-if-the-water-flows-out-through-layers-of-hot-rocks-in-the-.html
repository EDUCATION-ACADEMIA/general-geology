Hot spring water will be produced if the water flows out through layers of hot rocks in the ____

(A) crust

(B) ** mantle

(C) outer core

(D) inner core

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Magma is molten rock under Earth’s surface. Magma heats the water causing it to expand and become less dense. This hot, less dense water then rises through cracks and faults in Earth’s crust forming hot springs.

Concept note-2: -Subsurface magma heats groundwater, creating steam and hot water. The hot, less dense water rises through fissures and cracks in the ground. When it reaches the surface, features such as geysers, fumaroles, hot springs, and mud pits are created.

Concept note-3: -Geysers are hot springs that intermittently spout a column of hot water and steam into the air. This action is caused by the water in deep conduits beneath a geyser approaching or reaching the boiling point.

Concept note-4: -A hot spring is a discharge of hot (>35–40°C) water from a vent at the Earth’s surface. A geyser is a hot spring characterized by intermittent, turbulent discharges of boiling water and steam. A sublacustrine hot spring is a hot spring that discharges from the floor of a lake.

Concept note-5: -A hot spring, hydrothermal spring, or geothermal spring is a spring produced by the emergence of geothermally heated groundwater onto the surface of the Earth. The groundwater is heated either by shallow bodies of magma (molten rock) or by circulation through faults to hot rock deep in the Earth’s crust.
