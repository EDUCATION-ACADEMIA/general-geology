What is the thinnest layer of the Earth?

(A) Mantle

(B) ** Crust

(C) Inner Core

(D) Outer Core

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Oceanic Crust is the thinnest layer of earth.

Concept note-2: -The Earth is covered by two kinds of crust-continental and oceanic. The thinner oceanic crust is normally a little more than four miles thick, while the thicker continental crust is often as much as 25 miles thick. Continental crust is also much less dense than its oceanic counterpart.

Concept note-3: -The term soil refers to the soft, thin layer on the earthâ€™s surface on which plants grow. Soil contains both organic and inorganic substances in large quantity. Soils are generally formed through the breaking down of rocks.

Concept note-4: -There are two main types of crust; continental crust and oceanic crust that differ in a number of ways. Continental crust is thicker than oceanic crust, averaging 20-70 km thick, compared to 5-10 km for oceanic crust.

Concept note-5: -In relation to the other layers the crust is the thinnest and the least dense layer. It floats on the softer, denser mantle. The crust is made up of solid rock but these rocks are not the same all over the world.
