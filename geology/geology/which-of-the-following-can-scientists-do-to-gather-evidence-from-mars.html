Which of the following can scientists do to gather evidence from Mars?

(A) Scientists cannot gather evidence from Mars until they travel there.

(B) ** Observe photos from Mars and compare landforms to Earth.

(C) ** Scientists can compare the Mars System and compare it to our Earth System.

(D) ** Observe photos from other rocky planets and compare them to Mars.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Geologists are scientists who study a planet’s solid features, like soil, rocks, and minerals. There are all kinds of rocks and minerals that make up our planet – as well as the Moon, Mars, and other rocky worlds.

Concept note-2: -The Mars Science Laboratory is studying carbon and water cycles on the planet through its history. That is, it seeks to determine in what form and amount carbon and water is stored on the planet or in its atmosphere and how that may have changed through time.

Concept note-3: -Previous missions to the Red Planet investigated its surface by studying its canyons, volcanoes, rocks, and soil. But the signatures of the planet’s formation can only be found by sensing and studying its vital signs far below the surface.

Concept note-4: -When the rover speaks directly to Earth (from the surface of Mars), it sends messages via its high-gain antenna (HGA). The high-gain antenna can send a “beam” of information in a specific direction and it is steerable, so the antenna can move to point itself directly to any antenna on Earth.
