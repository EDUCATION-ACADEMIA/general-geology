Copper Penny has a hardness of

(A) 3

(B) ** 3.5

(C) 5

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On the Mohs scale, fingernail has hardness 2.5; copper penny, about 3.5; a knife blade, 5.5; window glass, 5.5; steel file, 6.5. Using these ordinary materials of known hardness can be a simple way to approximate the position of a mineral on the scale.

Concept note-2: -With a hardness of approximately 3, a copper penny easily scratches a specimen of gypsum (hardness 2).
