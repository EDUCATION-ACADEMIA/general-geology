mountains generally formed when magma pushes upward into Earth’s crust form the mantle and cools into igneous rock underground, causing the crust above it to bulge; usually occur as isolated mountains on otherwise flat plains

(A) fold mountains

(B) fault-block mountains

(C) ** dome mountains

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When magma pushes the crust up but hardens before erupting onto the surface, it forms so-called dome mountains.

Concept note-2: -Dome Mountains Dome mountains are formed when the magma, which is a hot layer of rock, pushes up through the lithosphere, causing the crust to bend up.

Concept note-3: -Volcanic mountains: these mountain ranges are formed when molten rock, or magma, rises to the surface and solidifies, creating a cone-shaped mountain. Examples of volcanic mountains include Mount St. Helens in Washington and Mount Fuji in Japan.

Concept note-4: -Dome Mountains are formed from hot molten material (magma) rising from the Earth’s mantle into the crust that pushes overlying sedimentary rock layers upward to form a “dome” shape.
