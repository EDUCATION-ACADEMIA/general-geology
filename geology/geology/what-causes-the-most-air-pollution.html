What causes the MOST air pollution?

(A) Acid rain

(B) Pollen

(C) ** Burning fossil fuels

(D) Landslides

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When fossil fuels are burned, they release nitrogen oxides into the atmosphere, which contribute to the formation of smog and acid rain. Major sources of nitrogen oxide emissions include: Cars and trucks. Coal-fired power plants.

Concept note-2: -Burning Fossil Fuels The biggest contributors of air pollution are from industry sources and power plants to generate power, as well as fossil fuel motor vehicles. The continuous burning of fossil fuels releases air pollutants, emissions and chemicals into the air and atmosphere.

Concept note-3: -When fossil fuels are burnt, it produces oxides of nitrogen and sulphur. These gases cause inhalation problems and in presence of rain forms acid rain. The burning of fossil fuels also increases the amount of suspended particles in the air that reduce the visibility.

Concept note-4: -Producing and burning fossil fuels creates air pollution that harms our health and generates toxic emissions that drive climate change. From the electricity that lights our homes to the cars we drive to work, modern life was built on fossil fuels like coal, oil and natural gas.
