The term “greenhouse effect” refers to an increase in atmosphere temperatures resulting from an increasing blanket of clouds around the earth.

(A) True

(B) ** False

(C) Dont know

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The term “greenhouse effect” refers to an increase in atmospheric temperatures resulting from an increasing blanket of clouds around the earth. The principal source of rising levels of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere is the burning of fossil fuels.

Concept note-2: -The greenest effect causes energy to be reflected from the surface of the earth back into space, so this is a false statement. This is our answer.

Concept note-3: -The greenhouse effect is the way in which heat is trapped close to Earth’s surface by “greenhouse gases.” These heat-trapping gases can be thought of as a blanket wrapped around Earth, keeping the planet toastier than it would be without them.

Concept note-4: -When the Earth’s surface is heated upon by the radiations from the Earth, it results in increased temperature than is generally required, this process is known as the greenhouse effect.

Concept note-5: -Swedish physicist and physical chemist Svante Arrhenius is credited with the origins of the term in 1896, with the publication of the first plausible climate model that explained how gases in Earth’s atmosphere trap heat.
