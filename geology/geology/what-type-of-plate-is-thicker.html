What type of plate is thicker?

(A) ** continental

(B) oceanic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Continental crust is typically 40 km (25 miles) thick, while oceanic crust is much thinner, averaging about 6 km (4 miles) in thickness.

Concept note-2: -Oceanic plates (50-100km) are thinner than the continental plates (up to 200km) and even thinner at the ocean ridges where the temperatures are higher.

Concept note-3: -Continental plates are thicker and less dense than oceanic plates that are formed at spreading ridges.

Concept note-4: -Earth is covered by two different kinds of crust: oceanic and continental crust. The normal oceanic crust is  7 km thick (White et al., 1992), whereas the continental crust is  40 km thick on average (Christensen and Mooney, 1995).
