What is evidence?

(A) A question about the natural world

(B) An answer to a question about the natural world

(C) ** Information or proof about something in the natural world

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In scientific research evidence is accumulated through observations of phenomena that occur in the natural world, or which are created as experiments in a laboratory or other controlled conditions.

Concept note-2: -Science is an organized way of using evidence to learn about the natural world. Scientific thinking begins with observation. Observation is the process of gathering information about events or processes in a careful, orderly way. The information gathered from observations is called data.

Concept note-3: -A theory is a well-substantiated explanation of an aspect of the natural world that can incorporate laws, hypotheses and facts.

Concept note-4: -Evidence can be defined as proof, available body of facts and information, to indicate whether a belief or hypothesis is justified. The natural sciences and history are two such areas of knowledge where evidence is essential validate a claim.

Concept note-5: -The scientific method is a systematic process that scientists have developed to discover the principles of the natural world. It is a complex and variable process that differs in detail from scientists to scientists and discovery to discovery.
