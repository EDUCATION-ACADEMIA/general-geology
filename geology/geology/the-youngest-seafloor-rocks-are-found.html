The youngest seafloor rocks are found:

(A) ** near the rift valleys of the mid-ocean ridges.

(B) evenly distributed over the ocean basins.

(C) nearest the continental slopes.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The youngest rocks in the ocean floor are located at the mid-ocean ridges. Although the Mid-Atlantic Ridge is mostly an underwater feature, portions of it, such as the island of Iceland, have enough elevation to extend above sea level. a rift valley is formed when continental plates pull apart by normal faults.

Concept note-2: -The youngest seafloor rocks can be found on mid-ocean ridges, which form part of the ocean crust. Mid-ocean ridges are dynamic geographic features located in places where the Earthâ€™s plates are pulling away from one another; they are continually producing new rocks.

Concept note-3: -Scientists found that the youngest rocks are located at the mid-ocean ridges. The ages of the rocks become increasingly older in samples obtained farther from the ridges, adding to the evidence for seafloor spreading.

Concept note-4: -The material that erupts at spreading centers along the mid-ocean ridge is primarily basalt, the most common rock on Earth.

Concept note-5: -A rift valley split the two continental crust apart forming an ocean between them. The continental shelf is where the new ocean started. This is where the oldest ocean crust exists.
