The rock cycle shows how:

(A) metamorphic rocks are formed from sedimentary rocks, which are formed from igneous rocks.

(B) weathering and erosion eventually breaks down all rocks and one day there won’t be any rocks

(C) ** the Earth’s minerals are continually recycled between igneous, sedimentary and metamorphic rocks.

(D) sedimentary, igneous and metamorphic rocks are formed from unrelated processes.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The interaction between the tectonic and the hydrologic systems causes constant recycling of the materials of the Earth’s crust. Rocks are heated, metamorphosed, melted, weathered, sediment is transported, deposited and lithified, then it may be metamorphosed again in yet another cycle.

Concept note-2: -Igneous rock can change into sedimentary rock or into metamorphic rock. Sedimentary rock can change into metamorphic rock or into igneous rock. Metamorphic rock can change into igneous or sedimentary rock. Igneous rock forms when magma cools and makes crystals.

Concept note-3: -Igneous rocks can become sedimentary rocks through weathering and erosion. Igneous rocks can become metamorphic rocks through intense heat and pressure. Igneous rocks can melt to magma, and crystallize as different igneous rocks.

Concept note-4: -The rock cycle is a web of processes that outlines how each of the three major rock types-igneous, metamorphic, and sedimentary-form and break down based on the different applications of heat and pressure over time. For example, sedimentary rock shale becomes slate when heat and pressure are added.
