Radiometric dating is possible because the rates of decay of radioactive isotopes ____ .

(A) change over time

(B) change from place to place

(C) ** are constant

(D) vary widely

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Radiometric dating is possible because the rates of decay of radioactive isotopes. They use absolute radiometric dating requires extremely long-lived parent nucleus this requires extremely long-lived parent isotope. One subset of a method for rocks.

Concept note-2: -The rate of decay remains constant throughout the decay process. There are three ways to show the exponential nature of half-life. Equation 11 is a constant, meaning the half-life of radioactive decay is constant.

Concept note-3: -The carbon-14 dating limit lies around 58, 000 to 62, 000 years. The rate of creation of carbon-14 appears to be roughly constant, as cross-checks of carbon-14 dating with other dating methods show it gives consistent results.

Concept note-4: -The basic logic behind radiometric dating is that if you compare the presence of a radioactive isotope within a sample to its known abundance on Earth, and its known half-life (its rate of decay), you can calculate the age of the sample.

Concept note-5: -Another important atomic clock used for dating purposes is based on the radioactive decay of the isotope carbon-14, which has a half-life of 5, 730 years.
