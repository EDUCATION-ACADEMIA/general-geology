What is an eruption of a column of steam and hot water that blasts through cracks in the crust?

(A) Tsunami

(B) Volcano

(C) Earthquake

(D) ** Geyser

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sometimes, the magma heats up groundwater under the Earthâ€™s surface, which causes water and steam to erupt like a volcano. These eruptions are called geysers.

Concept note-2: -Geyser eruptions are driven by the conversion of thermal to kinetic energy during decompression. In other words, water deep in the ground is heated up by nearby hot rocks, and when conditions are just right, and the pressure of the overlying rocks is released, the water will erupt out of the ground as a geyser.

Concept note-3: -The most likely explosive event to occur at Yellowstone is actually a hydrothermal explosion (a rock hurling geyser eruption) or a lava flow. Hydrothermal explosions are very small; they occur in Yellowstone National Park every few years and form a crater a few meters across.

Concept note-4: -Hydrothermal explosions are violent and dramatic events resulting in the rapid ejection of boiling water, steam, mud, and rock fragments. The explosions can reach heights of 2 km (1.2 miles) and leave craters that are from a few meters (tens of feet) up to more than 2 km (1.2 mi) in diameter.

Concept note-5: -Subsurface magma heats groundwater, creating steam and hot water. The hot, less dense water rises through fissures and cracks in the ground. When it reaches the surface, features such as geysers, fumaroles, hot springs, and mud pits are created.
