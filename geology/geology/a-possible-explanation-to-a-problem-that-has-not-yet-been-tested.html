A possible explanation to a problem that has not yet been tested.

(A) data

(B) theory

(C) ** hypothesis

(D) fact

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Hypothesis: A possible explanation to the question or problem. It is simply a prediction and has not yet been proven or disproven.

Concept note-2: -A hypothesis is a possible explanation for a set of observations or an answer to a scientific question. A hypothesis must be testable and measurable. This means that researchers must be able to carry out investi-gations and gather evidence that will either support or disprove the hypothesis.

Concept note-3: -The term for an explanation for an observation or scientific problem that can be tested by further investigation is a hypothesis. A hypothesis makes an attempt to explain a potential solution to the question asked, and it must be testable so that the investigation can be performed.

Concept note-4: -A possible explanation about an observation that can be tested by scientific investigations is a hypothesis. A prediction is a statement of what will happen next in a sequence of events.

Concept note-5: -Non-experimental hypothesis tests In situations like these, biologists may use non-experimental forms of hypothesis testing. In a non-experimental hypothesis test, a researcher predicts observations or patterns that should be seen in nature if the hypothesis is correct.
