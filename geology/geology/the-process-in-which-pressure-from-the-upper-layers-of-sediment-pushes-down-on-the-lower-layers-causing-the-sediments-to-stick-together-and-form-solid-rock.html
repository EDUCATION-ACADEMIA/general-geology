The process in which pressure from the upper layers of sediment pushes down on the lower layers, causing the sediments to stick together and form solid rock.

(A) ** Compaction

(B) marble

(C) coal

(D) limestone

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pressure from the upper layers pushes down on the lower layers. 2. If the sediments are small, they can stick together and form solid rock. This process is called compaction.

Concept note-2: -17) compaction process that forms sedimentary rocks when layers of sediment are compressed by the layers above them. The pressure from the upper layers of sediment pushes down on the lower layers of sediment causing the sediments to stick together and form solid rock.

Concept note-3: -Finally, lithification is the process by which clay, sand, and other sediments on the bottom of the ocean or other bodies of water are slowly compacted into rocks from the weight of overlying sediments.

Concept note-4: -Lithification (Diagenesis)-Lithification is the process that turns sediment into rock. The first stage of the process is compaction. Compaction occurs as the weight of the overlying material increases. Compaction forces the grains closer together, reducing pore space and eliminating some of the contained water.
