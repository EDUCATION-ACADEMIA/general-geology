The undisturbed upper layer of rocks usually contain fossils of organisms that are

(A) ** More complex that those found in the lower layers.

(B) less complex than those found in lower layers.

(C) indentical to those found in the lower layers

(D) different from any other fossils in the lower layers.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Almost all fossils are preserved in sedimentary rock.

Concept note-2: -Superposition. Sedimentary rocks are deposited one on top of another. Therefore, the youngest layers are found at the top, and the oldest layers are found at the bottom of the sequence.

Concept note-3: -Rock layers are also called strata (the plural form of the Latin word stratum), and stratigraphy is the science of strata.

Concept note-4: -Sediment accumulates in horizontal layers to form sedimentary rock. According to the principle of superposition, in undisturbed layers of rock, the oldest rocks are on the bottom.
