The magnetic striping of the seafloor is considered evidence of seafloor spreading and:

(A) spreading centers in the trenches.

(B) ** periodic reversals in the polarity of the Earth’s magnetic field.

(C) changes in the Earth’s axis of rotation.

(D) subduction down the rift valleys.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The magnetic striping of the seafloor is considered evidence of seafloor spreading and: periodic reversals in the polarity of the Earth’s magnetic field. Geologists believe that a new ocean basin will form: along the East African divergent rift system.

Concept note-2: -When the Earth’s magnetic field reverses, a new stripe, with the new polarity, begins. Such magnetic patterns led to recognition of the occurrence of sea-floor spreading, and they remain some of the strongest evidence for the theory of plate tectonics.

Concept note-3: -These patterns of stripes provide the history of seafloor spreading. Geophysicists can read these patterns from the magnetic anomalies they measure with a magnetometer. Where the magnetic wiggles, or anomalies, are broader, the spreading rate has been faster.

Concept note-4: -Magnetic striping is a pattern of magnetic fields in strips of rock as they radiate outward from an ocean ridge. What happens here is that, near the ocean ridge itself, we have younger rock that was more recently formed from magma that is emitted through cracks in the Earth’s crust.

Concept note-5: -The striped magnetic pattern develops because, as oceanic crust pulls apart, magma rises to the surface at mid-ocean ridges and spills out to create new bands of ocean floor.
