What does a planet need in order to be habitable?

(A) Water

(B) Source of energy

(C) ** Both A + B

(D) Houses

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What makes the Earth habitable? It is the right distance from the Sun, it is protected from harmful solar radiation by its magnetic field, it is kept warm by an insulating atmosphere, and it has the right chemical ingredients for life, including water and carbon.

Concept note-2: -The availability of liquid water is the most important factor that makes a planet habitable, because water is a very effective polar molecule and hence an excellent solvent and facilitator for the complex chemistry of life.

Concept note-3: -A “habitable” planet should: Orbit a star that remains stable in output for billions of years. Be at a distance from the star that results in its achieving a suitable temperature so its surface water is liquid, not frozen. Have a circular orbit, so constant conditions prevail for its entire “year"

Concept note-4: -Right distance from their star. More or less circular orbit. Stable rotational axis. Some water, but not too much. No hydrogen-rich atmosphere. Plate tectonics. Magnetic field. 23-Apr-2021
