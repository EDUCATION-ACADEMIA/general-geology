The channel on Mars is important because of the potential of

(A) flowing lava

(B) ** flowing water

(C) flowing air

(D) beautiful flowing hair on Mr. Red’s head

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -› Larger image Flowing lava can carve or build paths very much like the riverbeds and canyons etched by water, and this probably explains at least one of the meandering channels on the surface of Mars.

Concept note-2: -Understanding the extent and situation of water on Mars is vital to assess the planet’s potential for harboring life and for providing usable resources for future human exploration.

Concept note-3: -Mars once had flowing water that carved the channels, but there is no evidence of water on the surface today.

Concept note-4: -Liquid water appears to flow from some steep, relatively warm slopes on the Martian surface. Features known as recurring slope lineae (RSL) were first identified in 2011in images taken by the High Resolution Imaging Science Experiment (HiRISE) camera aboard the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO).
