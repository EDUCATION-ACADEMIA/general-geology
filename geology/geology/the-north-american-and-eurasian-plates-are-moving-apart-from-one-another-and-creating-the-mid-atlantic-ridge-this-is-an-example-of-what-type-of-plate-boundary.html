The North American and Eurasian plates are moving apart from one another and creating the Mid Atlantic ridge. This is an example of what type of plate boundary?

(A) Covergent, such as the plate movement that causes earthquakes in Japan and the Philippines.

(B) ** Divergent, such as between the African and Arabian plates.

(C) Subduction, such as along the West Coast of South America.

(D) Convection, which causes molten rock beneath the surface to move in opposite directions.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Divergent boundaries. Divergent boundaries occur along spreading centers where plates are moving apart and new crust is created by magma pushing up from the mantle.

Concept note-2: -The North American Plate forms a divergent boundary with the Eurasian Plate and a transform boundary with the Pacific Plate along the coast of California.

Concept note-3: -A divergent boundary occurs when two tectonic plates move away from each other. Along these boundaries, earthquakes are common and magma (molten rock) rises from the Earthâ€™s mantle to the surface, solidifying to create new oceanic crust. The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is an example of divergent plate boundaries.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The plate boundary between the Eurasian Plate and Indian Plate is a convergent boundary.
