According to the selection, why has “the Big Bend” been described as a “geologist’s paradise”?

(A) A- because it contains rocks

(B) B- because many geologists vacation there

(C) ** C- because sparse vegetation allows easy access to interesting strata

(D) D- because there are many jobs for geologists there

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -That portion of the earth’s surface known as “the Big Bend” has often been described as a “geologist’s paradise.” In part this is due to the sparse vegetation of the region, which allows the various strata to be easily observed and studied.

Concept note-2: -It states that layers of rock strata at different locations can be correlated according to the unique set of fossils they contain.

Concept note-3: -Big Bend National Park has often been described as a “geologist’s paradise, ” but it has also been called a “geologist’s nightmare.” The visitor can readily observe rock strata exposed at odd angles, standing vertical, or turned completely upside down.

Concept note-4: -Radiometric dating. Most absolute dates for rocks are obtained with radiometric methods. These use radioactive minerals in rocks as geological clocks. The atoms of some chemical elements have different forms, called isotopes.

Concept note-5: -It is about 4/100ths of the span of geologic time on Earth. Which factor is a challenge to geologists in establishing the boundaries of the geologic time scale? Most rocks do not contain minerals that can be dated using radiometric dating.
